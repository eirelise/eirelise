Explore New Zealand


New Zealand has many unique attractions, including many different national parks. Here's all the best places and attractions to which you can visit and explore!

Fiordland National Park

Also referred to as the eighth wonder of the world. This famous park is a popular tourist’s choice, anyone would be sad to miss out on the wonderful views you can get. Fiordland can be explored in many different ways, so you can gain many new and incredible experiences from the same place.

No entrance fee
Official site

Aoraki / Mount Cook National Park

Sitting at a whopping 3724 meters high, Aoraki is New Zealand's tallest peak. Even though this mountain might be tall, it doesn’t mean newbies can't walk on it! The Mount Cook walk’s distance is about 10km long, so it can take around 2-4 hours including the return too. For the more experienced people, you can choose to HIKE this masterpiece, which can take from 12-16 hours alone or more in groups. Although the climb might be physically demanding the adventure will definitely still be worth it!

No entrance fee

Tongariro National Park

Located in the heart of the north island is where you can find Tongariro national park sitting on 3 whole volcanos. This is one unmissable site as you can be a witness to the wonders of the volcanos. The Tongariro alpine crossing lasts from 6 to 8 hours and is considered difficult, this path which stretches to around 20 kilometers can give you an unforgettable impression.

No entrance fee
Official site

Abel Tasman National Park

The Abel Tasman National Park is well known for its extraordinary golden beaches with clear waters and spectacular marine life. This area is perfect for those who have a passion for kayaking. Abel Tasman National Park is flawless due to the variety of trails it offers. The most famous Abel Tasman Track, takes you along the coastline so you can come across and slowly enjoy this lush scenery.

No entrance fee
Official site

Kaikoura Marine

Kaikoura is a mesmerizing coastal town famed for the remarkable sea life that inhabits the water. There are many who wish to see and encounter numerous sea creatures, such as sperm whales, dolphins and cute seals can be found here! You can meet these stunning animals in a boat or kayak and even from above in a plane!

And with a beautiful and cultured town such as Kaikoura, also comes many different restaurants, cafes and many shops close by! This is the ideal spot to go around and explore, and come back to eat and sleep in a nearby hotel.

This is an exquisite outing for those who are in love with these sweet and lovable sea creatures.

Costs around 100-200$ or more
See more on this site.

Willowbank Wildlife Reserve

So many eye-catching animals are waiting for your arrival, Willowbank Wildlife Reserve can show you the most glorious critters and creatures. With a lot of unique animals such as the famous Kiwi, adorable otters and the most charming red tailed black cockatoo, and that's not even a start on how many animals you can visit! In the reserve you can find many of New Zealand's animals. There’s so much to see and experience. The reserve does also include a cafe and restaurant, so no need to go hungry!

What a perfect spot to go with your family!

Tickets cost 13-36$ per person depending on age group
Official site

The Hobbiton Movie Set

Auckland is one of the most beautiful places in New Zealand. Though the beaches in Auckland are pretty, they are not the mouth-dropping sight, it is rather the amazingly built Hobbiton Movie Set which still stands after the completion of the movies. Now you can journey through this magical world and experience how it is for those who inhabit that world. And even though you haven’t watched the movies or read the books, you can still enjoy this experience.

Enjoy this wonderful 2.5 hour tour with family and friends!

Adults 120$, youth (ages 11-17) 60$, ages 0-10 free
Official site

Waitomo Caves

In Auckland there’s an exquisite village called Waitomo, which is home to extensive underground cave systems. These cave systems are a home to hordes of glowworms which light the caves up in a magical way! This is one of the most impactful places which leaves an imprint on you while on your boat journey. Visit and become a part of the 130 year old cultural and natural history of these lit up caves!

Adults 80$. Children 40$
Official site
